Legislative Transparency Platform

Live

A civic engagement tool that automates Congressional bill tracking with AI-powered summaries, making legislative activity accessible and understandable. Bills are ingested daily from Congress.gov, summarized by Claude API, and surfaced through a clean public interface.

Browse by Status

Bills organized by legislative status with real-time counts, from introduction through committee to floor votes and signing.

AI-Powered Summaries

Claude API generates two-tier summaries: key points at a glance plus expandable detail for deeper reading.

From Congress.gov to Plain English

Raw legislative data transformed into readable, searchable bill cards covering diverse policy areas.

The platform runs on Flask and PostgreSQL, deployed on Railway with scheduled jobs for daily bill ingestion from Congress.gov. Key design decisions included a two-tier summary format to balance accessibility with depth, rate limiting and retry logic for reliable API interactions, and a multi-dimensional reviewer feedback system so human oversight can continuously improve summary quality. API cost monitoring was built in from the start to track Claude API usage and ensure sustainable operation at scale.

Solar Energy Intelligence

Live

A real-time monitoring and rate optimization dashboard for residential solar, tracking production, consumption, credit balances, and projected ROI. Designed to scale across any residential installation, it bridges the gap between raw inverter data and the financial picture that homeowners care about.

Real-Time Monitoring

Production and consumption data updated every 15 minutes from the SolarEdge API, with toggleable daily, weekly, and monthly views.

Credit Tracking & ROI

Net metering credits tracked with FIFO expiration logic under Maine's 12-month NEB policy, alongside cumulative savings and payback progress.

Rate Plan Optimization

Monthly bill modeled across CMP's Rate A, Electric Technology, and Time-of-Use plans to identify the most cost-effective structure.

Automated 15-minute data collection from the SolarEdge API which is combined with utility billing records to model energy costs across three CMP rate structures. Credit tracking uses FIFO queue logic to match Maine's 12-month rolling expiration policy under the Net Energy Billing program. The rate plan comparison reveals whether Rate A, Electric Technology, or Time-of-Use pricing is most favorable given a site's actual production and consumption patterns.
